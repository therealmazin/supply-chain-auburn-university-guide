# Further Reading

Want to learn more? Here are recommended resources.

## AI and Machine Learning

### For Beginners

- **[Google's Machine Learning Crash Course](https://developers.google.com/machine-learning/crash-course)** - Free, visual, beginner-friendly
- **[Kaggle Learn](https://www.kaggle.com/learn)** - Short courses with hands-on exercises
- **[3Blue1Brown Neural Networks](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDNU6R1_67000Dx_ZCJB-3pi)** - Visual explanations of how ML works

### For Going Deeper

- **"Hands-On Machine Learning" by Aurélien Géron** - The standard ML textbook
- **[Fast.ai](https://www.fast.ai/)** - Practical deep learning course
- **[StatQuest](https://www.youtube.com/user/joshstarmer)** - Statistics explained clearly

## Supply Chain Analytics

### Industry Reports

- **McKinsey: "Supply Chain 4.0"** - How AI is transforming supply chains
- **Gartner: "Predicts 2024"** - Future of supply chain technology
- **APICS/ASCM Reports** - Professional supply chain insights

### Academic Resources

- **MIT Supply Chain Management** - Research and courses
- **CSCMP (Council of Supply Chain Management Professionals)** - Industry resources

## Claude and AI Assistants

### Official Documentation

- **[Claude Documentation](https://docs.anthropic.com/)** - Official Anthropic docs
- **[Claude Code Guide](https://docs.anthropic.com/en/docs/claude-code)** - How to use Claude Code effectively
- **[Prompt Engineering Guide](https://docs.anthropic.com/en/docs/prompt-engineering)** - Write better prompts

### Best Practices

- **Be specific** - Tell Claude exactly what you want
- **Provide context** - Explain what you're trying to accomplish
- **Iterate** - Refine your prompts based on results
- **Verify** - Don't blindly trust outputs

## Recommended Books

### Supply Chain

- **"The Goal" by Eliyahu Goldratt** - Classic operations management
- **"Supply Chain Management" by Chopra & Meindl** - Textbook standard
- **"Logistics & Supply Chain Management" by Martin Christopher** - Practical focus

### Business + AI

- **"Prediction Machines" by Agrawal, Gans, Goldfarb** - Economics of AI
- **"AI Superpowers" by Kai-Fu Lee** - Global AI landscape
- **"Human + Machine" by Daugherty & Wilson** - AI in the enterprise

## Keep Practicing

The best way to learn is by doing:

1. **Use Claude Code regularly** - Build the habit
2. **Try different datasets** - Find public data on Kaggle
3. **Work on real problems** - Ask professors for project data
4. **Join competitions** - Kaggle competitions are great practice
